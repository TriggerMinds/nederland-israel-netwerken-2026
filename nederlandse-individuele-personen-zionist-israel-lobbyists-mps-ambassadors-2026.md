## Executive Summary

As of mid-2026, the Dutch political and diplomatic landscape features a highly coordinated network of individual public figures—Members of Parliament (MPs), diplomats, and professional lobbyists—actively advocating for pro-Israel policies. This network operates through informal parliamentary groups, influential think tanks (like CIDI and thinc.), and significant financial structures (ANBI foundations). The ecosystem faces unprecedented scrutiny, largely driven by Freedom of Information (Woo) requests from groups like The Rights Forum, intense academic boycotts, and a landmark 2025 National Coordinator for Security and Counterterrorism (NCTV) report identifying direct Israeli state interference in Dutch domestic politics. This document provides an uncensored mapping of these individuals and their connections.

## 1. Members of Parliament (MPs) & Parliamentary Networks

While the Dutch parliament lacks a formal, universally recognized "Israel friendship group," coordination is heavily driven by the **Israel Allies Foundation (IAF)** and sympathetic party factions (primarily SGP, ChristenUnie, PVV, BBB, VVD, and JA21).

### Key Individuals
*   **Chris Stoffer (SGP):** A prominent figure in the Israel Allies Foundation. Consistently advocates for the recognition of Jerusalem as Israel's capital and strongly opposes funding for UNRWA.
*   **Don Ceder (ChristenUnie):** Active in pro-Israel parliamentary initiatives, often bridging the gap between Christian Zionist principles and formal foreign policy.
*   **Claudia van Zanten (BBB):** Has taken strong public stances defending Israeli defense actions and advocating against academic boycotts.
*   **Raymond de Roon (PVV):** A long-standing, highly vocal supporter of Israel within the PVV, frequently framing the conflict in terms of civilizational struggle and security against Islamic extremism.
*   **Ruben Baumgarten & Annabel Nanninga (JA21 - Senate):** Key figures in the Senate utilizing CIDI and NCAB talking points to push for institutionalizing the IHRA definition of antisemitism, often utilizing it to combat anti-Zionist activism.

## 2. Ambassadors and Senior Diplomats

The diplomatic channels serve as both the formal mechanism for bilateral relations and a frequent focal point for controversy.

### Key Individuals
*   **Zvi Aviner-Vapni (Israeli Ambassador to the Netherlands):** Appointed in September 2025. Highly vocal and active, particularly following violent incidents against Jewish communities in Amsterdam. He has been criticized for aggressive public diplomacy and bypassing traditional channels.
*   **Marriët Schuurman (Former Dutch Ambassador to Israel):** Central to diplomatic tensions in mid-2025 following the Dutch government's ban on Israeli Ministers Bezalel Smotrich and Itamar Ben-Gvir entering the Netherlands, which led to formal reprimands.
*   **Stella Grubacic-Ronner (Incoming Dutch Ambassador to Israel):** Nominated in early 2026, tasked with navigating the severely strained diplomatic relationship.
*   **Tom Berendsen (Dutch Foreign Minister):** Sworn in February 2026, overseeing the current diplomatic approach amidst intense pressure regarding arms exports and human rights compliance.

## 3. Lobbyists and Public Advocates

Professional lobbyists and advocates work to shape public opinion, parliamentary debates, and legal frameworks.

### Key Individuals & Organizations
*   **CIDI (Centrum Informatie en Documentatie Israël) Leadership:** The most powerful pro-Israel lobby group in the Netherlands. Directly provides "talking points" to center-right MPs.
*   **Eddo Verdoner:** First coordinator of the **NCAB (National Coordinator for Combating Antisemitism)** and former CIDI vice-chair. Has faced scrutiny from civil society for utilizing the NCAB office to push the IHRA definition, effectively conflating anti-Zionism with antisemitism in public policy.
*   **Esther Voet & Bart Schut (*NIW* - Nieuw Israëlitisch Weekblad):** While acting as journalists, they effectively function as specialized advocates, actively attacking progressive platforms (NRC, De Volkskrant) and the national broadcaster (NOS) for perceived anti-Israel bias.
*   **Andrew Tucker (thinc. - The Hague Initiative for International Cooperation):** Leads a legal think tank providing frameworks to MPs (especially SGP/PVV) to combat "lawfare" against Israel at the ICJ/ICC and defend against trade boycotts.

## 4. Connections & Networks: Funding, Think Tanks, and Defense

The individuals listed above do not operate in isolation; they are deeply embedded in institutional and financial networks.

### Financial Flows and Settlement Infrastructure
*   **Christian Zionist Funding:** Organizations like **Christenen voor Israël (CvI)** and **Stichting Ebenezer** maintain historic, symbiotic ties with ChristenUnie and SGP MPs. These groups funnel millions into Israel, including the controversial direct financing of infrastructure in illegal West Bank settlements.
*   **The Zuidas Network:** A network of letterbox foundations (ANBIs) in Amsterdam's financial district acts as a tax-efficient hub channeling international (often US) donations to Israeli settlement infrastructure and security equipment.
*   **JNF (Jewish National Fund) Nederland:** Faces severe legal scrutiny in 2026 regarding its tax-exempt (ANBI) status due to channeling funds into afforestation and land management in contested territories across the Green Line.

### Defense Contractors and "Dual-Use" Tech
*   **ELNET:** A powerful think tank that fiercely lobbies to defend the military-industrial relationship against boycotts.
*   **Corporate Links:** MPs and lobbyists actively defend companies like **GKN Fokker** (F-35 parts, partnered with Elbit Systems) and **Thales Nederland**. In late 2025, the Dutch MoD controversially purchased **SMASH 3000** anti-drone visors from the Israeli firm **Smartshooter**, heavily promoted by the defense lobby.
*   **Academic Dual-Use:** Technical universities (TU Delft, TU/e, UTwente) utilized EU Horizon Europe funds to partner with Israeli defense entities (Technion, IAI). Pressure from human rights groups forced the suspension of ties with Technion and Weebit Nano in late 2025/2026.

## 5. Controversies, Criticisms, and Pushback

The activities of these public figures and networks are highly contested, facing pushback from a broad coalition of actors.

### State Interference & The 2025 NCTV Report
In a major rupture in July 2025, the Dutch **NCTV (National Coordinator for Security and Counterterrorism)** officially named Israel as a state actor interfering in Dutch domestic affairs. The Israeli Ministry of Diaspora Affairs bypassed formal diplomatic channels (Ambassador Aviner-Vapni) to distribute a report directly to sympathetic Dutch MPs and journalists, attempting to frame Dutch pro-Palestinian groups for violence in Amsterdam. 

### The Rights Forum & "Woo-verzoek" Lawfare
The primary target of the pro-Israel diplomatic, lobbying, and media network is **The Rights Forum** (along with aligned NGOs like PAX and Plant een Olijfboom).
*   **Transparency Warfare:** The Rights Forum utilized Freedom of Information (Woo) requests to expose the hidden ties between Dutch universities, Israeli defense contractors, and pro-Israel advocacy groups (CIDI, NCAB). The pro-Israel lobby and sympathetic MPs fiercely attacked this FOIA campaign, framing it as an antisemitic witch-hunt against Jewish entities.
*   **Legal Action:** The Rights Forum successfully weaponized the Dutch legal system against the defense industry (forcing the 2025 F-35 export ban) and provided international law frameworks that fueled the massive academic boycotts of 2025-2026.
